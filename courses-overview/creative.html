NAU - Creative Writing

Creative Writing

Course Overview

The Creative Writing course at New Age University is designed to inspire and develop your writing skills across various genres. This course explores techniques for crafting compelling narratives, building characters, and mastering dialogue. Participants will engage in writing exercises, receive feedback on their work, and learn how to revise and polish their writing. Whether you're an aspiring author, a blogger, or someone who wants to express their creativity through words, this course will help you refine your voice and style.

Key Details

  • Duration: 6 months
  • Level: Beginner to Advance
  • Format: Online, Self-paced
  • Start Date: (Commig Soon!) July 13th, 2025
  • Cost: Free

Curriculum

The curriculum for this course includes the following modules, covering the essentials of creative writing and culminating in a capstone project in Week 10:

  • Week 1: Introduction to Creative Writing - Fundamentals of storytelling, creative expression, and the writing process.
  • Week 2: Developing Characters - Techniques for creating dynamic, memorable characters with depth and motivation.
  • Week 3: Crafting Dialogue - Writing realistic, engaging, and purposeful dialogue to reveal character and advance plot.
  • Week 4: Plot and Structure - Building cohesive story arcs, structuring narratives, and developing strong plotlines.
  • Week 5: Setting and World Building - Techniques for creating immersive settings and detailed worlds that enhance storytelling.
  • Week 6: Point of View and Voice - Exploring various narrative perspectives and developing a unique writing voice.
  • Week 7: Thematic Elements and Symbolism - Incorporating themes, symbols, and motifs to add layers of meaning to your work.
  • Week 8: Revising and Editing - Techniques for self-editing, refining prose, and preparing your writing for publication or presentation.
  • Week 9: Genre Exploration - Understanding different genres and writing styles, with exercises to experiment within various genres.
  • Week 10: Capstone Project - Completing and presenting a polished, original piece of creative writing that demonstrates skills learned throughout the course.
