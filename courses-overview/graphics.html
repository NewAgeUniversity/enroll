NAU - Graphic Design

Graphic Design

Course Overview

The Graphic Design course at New Age University provides a comprehensive introduction to the principles and practices of graphic design. This course covers essential design techniques, including layout, typography, color theory, and digital tools. Students will gain hands-on experience with industry-standard software and learn how to create visually compelling designs for various media, from print to digital platforms. Whether you're looking to start a career in graphic design or enhance your design skills, this course offers a solid foundation and practical insights.

Key Details

  • Duration: 6 months
  • Level: Beginner to Advance
  • Format: Online, Self-paced
  • Start Date: January 13th, 2025
  • Cost: Free

Curriculum

The curriculum for this course includes the following modules:

  • Week 1: Introduction to Graphic Design - Overview of graphic design principles and tools.
  • Week 2: Design Fundamentals - Basic design elements including line, shape, and form.
  • Week 3: Typography - Understanding fonts, typefaces, and text layout.
  • Week 4: Color Theory - Techniques for effective use of color in design.
  • Week 5: Layout and Composition - Designing effective layouts for print and digital media.
  • Week 6: Digital Design Tools - Introduction to software such as Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop.
  • Week 7: Branding and Identity - Creating logos, brand materials, and cohesive visual identities.
  • Week 8: Final Project - Applying design skills to a comprehensive project with real-world relevance.
