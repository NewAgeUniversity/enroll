Curriculum

The curriculum for this course includes the following modules:

  • Week 1: Introduction to Entrepreneurship - Understanding the entrepreneurial mindset, the importance of innovation, and the basics of starting a business.
  • Week 2: Identifying Opportunities - Techniques for discovering market gaps, assessing ideas, and validating business concepts.
  • Week 3: Business Models and Planning - Exploring different business models, creating a business plan, and understanding financial projections.
  • Week 4: Bootstrapping Your Startup - Strategies for launching a business with minimal resources, managing costs, and leveraging personal assets.
  • Week 5: Marketing Strategies for Startups - Developing effective marketing strategies, including digital marketing, social media, and content marketing for new businesses.
  • Week 6: Financial Management - Basics of accounting, budgeting, and financial forecasting for startups, along with funding options (loans, grants, investors).
  • Week 7: Building a Brand - Understanding brand identity, messaging, and customer experience; techniques for building a strong brand from scratch.
  • Week 8: Scaling Your Business - Strategies for growth, scaling operations, and managing resources effectively as your business expands.
  • Week 9: Legal Considerations for Startups - Overview of business structures, intellectual property, contracts, and compliance for entrepreneurs.
  • Week 10: Capstone Project - Develop a comprehensive business plan and pitch for a startup idea, showcasing the skills and knowledge gained throughout the course.

Gain essential entrepreneurship skills from ideation to execution, culminating in a hands-on capstone project that prepares you for the business world.
