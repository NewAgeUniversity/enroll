NAU - Critical Thinking

Critical Thinking

Course Overview

The Critical Thinking course at New Age University is designed to enhance students' analytical and reasoning skills. This course focuses on developing the ability to analyze arguments, identify logical fallacies, and make reasoned decisions. Participants will learn techniques to evaluate information critically and construct well-supported arguments. This course is essential for students and professionals looking to improve their problem-solving skills and make informed decisions in various aspects of life.

Key Details

  • Duration: 6 months
  • Level: Beginner to Advance
  • Format: Online, Self-paced
  • Start Date: (Commig Soon!) July 13th, 2025
  • Cost: Free

Curriculum

The curriculum for this course includes the following modules, designed to enhance critical thinking skills and culminate in a comprehensive final assessment:

  • Week 1: Introduction to Critical Thinking - Fundamentals of critical thinking, its importance, and its role in decision-making.
  • Week 2: Analyzing Arguments - Techniques for identifying, evaluating, and deconstructing arguments and evidence.
  • Week 3: Logical Fallacies - Overview of common logical fallacies, their impact on reasoning, and strategies to avoid them.
  • Week 4: Constructing Arguments - Strategies for building strong, well-reasoned arguments and effective persuasive techniques.
  • Week 5: Critical Thinking in Practice - Applying critical thinking skills to analyze case studies and real-world problems.
  • Week 6: Ethical Reasoning - Exploring ethical considerations in critical thinking and the importance of moral reasoning.
  • Week 7: Creative Problem Solving - Techniques for enhancing creativity in problem-solving and critical thinking processes.
  • Week 8: Group Discussions and Debates - Engaging in structured discussions and debates to practice critical thinking collaboratively.
  • Week 9: Review and Preparation - Recap of key concepts and skills, with preparation for the final assessment.
  • Week 10: Final Assessment - Comprehensive project or presentation demonstrating the application of critical thinking skills learned throughout the course.
