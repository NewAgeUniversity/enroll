World History

Course Overview

The World History course at New Age University offers a comprehensive exploration of significant events, civilizations, and cultural developments from ancient times to the modern era. This course aims to provide students with a deep understanding of how historical events have shaped the present world. Topics include the rise and fall of empires, major revolutions, and the evolution of societies and cultures. Ideal for history enthusiasts and students seeking to gain a broad perspective on global historical dynamics.

Key Details

  • Duration: 14 weeks
  • Level: Beginner to Intermediate
  • Format: Online, Self-paced
  • Start Date: December 1, 2024
  • Cost: Free

Curriculum

The curriculum for this course includes the following modules:

  • Week 1: Introduction to World History - Overview of historical periods and methodologies.
  • Week 2: Ancient Civilizations - Study of Mesopotamia, Egypt, Indus Valley, and ancient China.
  • Week 3: Classical Antiquity - Exploration of Greece, Rome, and the development of classical culture.
  • Week 4: The Middle Ages - Understanding the medieval period, including feudalism and the rise of kingdoms.
  • Week 5: The Renaissance and Reformation - Impact of Renaissance culture and the Protestant Reformation.
  • Week 6: Age of Exploration - Examination of global exploration and its consequences.
  • Week 7: The Enlightenment and Revolutions - Study of the Enlightenment era and major revolutions, including the American and French Revolutions.
  • Week 8: The Industrial Revolution - Impact of industrialization on societies and economies.
  • Week 9: The 20th Century - Key events such as World Wars, the Cold War, and decolonization.
  • Week 10: Modern Globalization - Understanding contemporary global issues and their historical roots.
  • Week 11: Regional Histories - In-depth study of specific regions such as Africa, Asia, and Latin America.
  • Week 12: Cultural Developments - Evolution of art, religion, and philosophy through history.
  • Week 13: Historical Methodologies - Approaches to historical research and analysis.
  • Week 14: Final Project - Integration of course materials into a comprehensive historical analysis.
