NAU - Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity

Course Overview

The Cybersecurity course at New Age University is designed to provide students with a thorough understanding of cybersecurity principles, techniques, and best practices. This course covers essential topics such as threat detection, risk management, and strategies for protecting information systems from cyberattacks. Students will gain hands-on experience with security tools, learn about current threats and vulnerabilities, and explore methods for safeguarding data and networks. Suitable for aspiring cybersecurity professionals and IT specialists, this course offers practical skills and knowledge to enhance your ability to defend against cyber threats.

Key Details

  • Duration: 6 months
  • Level: Beginner to Advance
  • Format: Online, Self-paced
  • Start Date: January 13th, 2025
  • Cost: Free

Curriculum

The curriculum for this course includes the following modules:

  • Week 1: Introduction to Cybersecurity - Fundamentals of cybersecurity, including concepts and terminology.
  • Week 2: Threats and Vulnerabilities - Types of cyber threats, common vulnerabilities, and attack vectors.
  • Week 3: Risk Management - Techniques for identifying, assessing, and mitigating cybersecurity risks.
  • Week 4: Network Security - Strategies for protecting networks from unauthorized access and attacks.
  • Week 5: Information Security - Safeguarding data through encryption, access controls, and secure communication.
  • Week 6: Security Tools and Technologies - Overview of tools used for threat detection, prevention, and response.
  • Week 7: Incident Response - Procedures for responding to and managing cybersecurity incidents.
  • Week 8: Final Project - Implementing a comprehensive cybersecurity strategy for a real or hypothetical scenario.
