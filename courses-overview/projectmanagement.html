NAU - Project Management

Project Management

Course Overview

The Project Management course at New Age University is designed to equip students with the skills and knowledge necessary to manage projects effectively from initiation to completion. This course covers key project management principles, methodologies, and tools, including project planning, scheduling, risk management, and stakeholder communication. Students will learn how to lead teams, manage resources, and ensure project success while meeting deadlines and staying within budget. Ideal for aspiring project managers and team leaders, this course provides practical insights and hands-on experience in project management.

Key Details

  • Duration: 6 months
  • Level: Beginner to Advance
  • Format: Online, Self-paced
  • Start Date: January 13th, 2025
  • Cost: Free

Curriculum

The curriculum for this course includes the following modules:

  • Week 1: Introduction to Project Management - Overview of project management processes and methodologies.
  • Week 2: Project Initiation - Defining project scope, objectives, and stakeholders.
  • Week 3: Project Planning - Developing project plans, timelines, and resource allocation.
  • Week 4: Scheduling and Budgeting - Techniques for creating schedules and managing project budgets.
  • Week 5: Risk Management - Identifying, analyzing, and mitigating project risks.
  • Week 6: Team Leadership - Leading and managing project teams effectively.
  • Week 7: Stakeholder Communication - Strategies for communicating with stakeholders and managing expectations.
  • Week 8: Final Project - Implementing a comprehensive project management plan for a real or hypothetical project.
