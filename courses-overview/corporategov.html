NAU - Corporate Governance

Corporate Governance

Course Overview

The Corporate Governance course at New Age University provides an in-depth understanding of the frameworks, principles, and practices that guide the governance of organizations. This course covers the roles and responsibilities of boards, executives, and shareholders, and explores best practices for ensuring transparency, accountability, and ethical behavior in corporate management. Ideal for current and aspiring board members, executives, and corporate managers, this course equips participants with the knowledge to foster strong governance structures and drive organizational success.

Key Details

  • Duration: 8 weeks
  • Level: Intermediate
  • Format: Online, Self-paced
  • Start Date: September 1, 2025
  • Cost: Free

Curriculum

The curriculum for this course includes the following modules:

  • Week 1: Introduction to Corporate Governance - Overview of governance structures and frameworks.
  • Week 2: Roles and Responsibilities - Understanding the roles of boards, executives, and shareholders.
  • Week 3: Governance Frameworks - Exploring various governance models and their applications.
  • Week 4: Regulatory Compliance - Navigating legal and regulatory requirements for corporate governance.
  • Week 5: Ethics and Corporate Social Responsibility - Integrating ethical practices and social responsibility into governance.
  • Week 6: Risk Management - Identifying and managing risks in corporate governance.
  • Week 7: Board Dynamics and Leadership - Enhancing board effectiveness and leadership skills.
  • Week 8: Final Project - Developing a comprehensive governance plan for a hypothetical organization.
